Greedy search is the simplest way for a generative AI model to pick the next word. 
It works by always choosing the single word that the model predicts has 
the highest probability at that exact moment.

The main problem is that this "greedy" approach 
often misses better overall sentences, as it doesn't look ahead.
It just picks the immediate best choice, which can lead to 
repetitive or less creative text, making it rarely used for 
practical applications like Smart Compose.


Greedy search is the simplest method for a generative AI model to create text. At each step, it simply picks the single word that has the highest probability of coming next.

Simple Example:

Imagine you're typing: "I want to eat a..."

The model might see these probabilities for the next word:

"pizza" (0.7 probability)
"burger" (0.2 probability)
"salad" (0.1 probability)
Greedy search immediately picks "pizza" because it has the highest probability.

Now the sentence is: "I want to eat a pizza." The model then predicts the next word for this sentence:

"tonight" (0.8 probability)
"with" (0.1 probability)
"later" (0.05 probability)
Greedy search picks "tonight". The final suggestion would be: "I want to eat a pizza tonight."

The Problem: While "pizza tonight" sounds fine, greedy search's flaw is that it only thinks one step ahead. It never reconsiders if choosing a slightly less probable word initially (like "burger") might have opened up a much better or more diverse sentence later on (e.g., "I want to eat a burger that is extra juicy"). This often leads to repetitive or generic suggestions, which is why it's rarely used in practice.
