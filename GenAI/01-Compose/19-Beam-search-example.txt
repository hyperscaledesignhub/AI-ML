Let's break down beam search with an easy example, using "I hope you are" as our starting partial email and a beam width (k) of 3.

Imagine you're trying to complete the sentence "I hope you are".

Step 1: Initialization
Input: Your partial email is "I hope you are".
Model Prediction: The model processes "I hope you are" and predicts probabilities for the very next word:
P("well" | "I hope you are") = 0.6
P("good" | "I hope you are") = 0.3
P("having" | "I hope you are") = 0.08
P("happy" | "I hope you are") = 0.05
... (and many other words with lower probabilities)
Beam Search Selects Top-k: Since our beam width is 3, we pick the top 3 most probable words:
Sequence 1: "I hope you are well" (Cumulative Probability: 0.6)
Sequence 2: "I hope you are good" (Cumulative Probability: 0.3)
Sequence 3: "I hope you are having" (Cumulative Probability: 0.08)
These are our initial 3 candidate paths.

Step 2: Expansion
Now, we extend each of these 3 sequences by one more word.

Extend Sequence 1: "I hope you are well" (Prob: 0.6)

Model predicts next words for "I hope you are well":
P("." | "I hope you are well") = 0.7
P("and" | "I hope you are well") = 0.2
P("today" | "I hope you are well") = 0.05
New potential longer sequences:
"I hope you are well ." (Cumulative: 0.6 * 0.7 = 0.42)
"I hope you are well and" (Cumulative: 0.6 * 0.2 = 0.12)
"I hope you are well today" (Cumulative: 0.6 * 0.05 = 0.03)
Extend Sequence 2: "I hope you are good" (Prob: 0.3)

Model predicts next words for "I hope you are good":
P("." | "I hope you are good") = 0.6
P("at" | "I hope you are good") = 0.3
P("with" | "I hope you are good") = 0.08
New potential longer sequences:
"I hope you are good ." (Cumulative: 0.3 * 0.6 = 0.18)
"I hope you are good at" (Cumulative: 0.3 * 0.3 = 0.09)
"I hope you are good with" (Cumulative: 0.3 * 0.08 = 0.024)
Extend Sequence 3: "I hope you are having" (Prob: 0.08)

Model predicts next words for "I hope you are having":
P("a" | "I hope you are having") = 0.8
P("fun" | "I hope you are having") = 0.1
P("some" | "I hope you are having") = 0.05
New potential longer sequences:
"I hope you are having a" (Cumulative: 0.08 * 0.8 = 0.064)
"I hope you are having fun" (Cumulative: 0.08 * 0.1 = 0.008)
"I hope you are having some" (Cumulative: 0.08 * 0.05 = 0.004)
Now we have a total of 9 potential sequences.

Step 3: Pruning
We look at all 9 newly generated sequences and their cumulative probabilities, and again, we select only the top 3 (our beam width).

Here are all 9 sequences and their probabilities, sorted:

"I hope you are well ." (0.42)
"I hope you are good ." (0.18)
"I hope you are well and" (0.12)
"I hope you are good at" (0.09)
"I hope you are having a" (0.064)
"I hope you are well today" (0.03)
"I hope you are good with" (0.024)
"I hope you are having fun" (0.008)
"I hope you are having some" (0.004)
The top 3 sequences (our new beam for the next iteration) are:

"I hope you are well ." (0.42)
"I hope you are good ." (0.18)
"I hope you are well and" (0.12)
This process (Expansion and Pruning) would repeat, generating one word at a time for each of the top 3 paths
, until a special "end of sequence" token (<EOS>) is predicted for all paths, or a maximum sentence length is reached. Finally, the path with the highest cumulative probability among the completed sentences would be chosen as the Smart Compose suggestion.
