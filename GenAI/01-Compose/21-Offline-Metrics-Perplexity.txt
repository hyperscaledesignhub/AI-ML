Perplexity is a metric used to evaluate language models that measures how
  well a model predicts a sequence of words. It quantifies the "surprise" or
  uncertainty of the model when encountering the actual text.

  Definition

  Perplexity is the exponential of the average negative log-likelihood of a
  sequence:

  Perplexity = exp(-1/N × Σ log P(wi | w1, w2, ..., wi-1))

  Where:
  - N = number of words in the sequence
  - P(wi | context) = probability the model assigns to word wi given the
  context
  - Lower perplexity = better model performance

  Intuitive Understanding

  - Low perplexity: Model is confident and accurate in its predictions
  - High perplexity: Model is uncertain and makes poor predictions
  - Perplexity of 1 = perfect prediction
  - Perplexity of V (vocabulary size) = random guessing

  Example

  Consider the sentence: "The cat sat on the mat"

  Good Model (assigns high probabilities):
  - P("The") = 0.8
  - P("cat" | "The") = 0.7
  - P("sat" | "The cat") = 0.6
  - P("on" | "The cat sat") = 0.8
  - P("the" | "The cat sat on") = 0.9
  - P("mat" | "The cat sat on the") = 0.5

  Average log-likelihood = -1/6 × (log(0.8) + log(0.7) + log(0.6) + log(0.8) +
  log(0.9) + log(0.5))
  = -1/6 × (-0.97) = 0.16
  Perplexity = exp(0.16) = 1.17

  Poor Model (assigns low probabilities):
  - Average probability per word = 0.1
  - Average log-likelihood = log(0.1) = -2.3
  - Perplexity = exp(2.3) = 10

  Practical Use

  - GPT models: Perplexity ~20-40 on common datasets
  - Translation: Lower perplexity correlates with better BLEU scores
  - Text generation: Helps choose between model architectures
  - Comparison: Only meaningful when comparing models on the same dataset

  Perplexity is particularly valuable for language modeling tasks like text
  generation, machine translation, and speech recognition.
