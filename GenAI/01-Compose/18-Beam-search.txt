Beam search is a smarter way to generate text than greedy search. Instead of just picking the single most probable next word, it keeps track of the top-k most likely sequences (where 'k' is the "beam width") at each step.

Here's the simplified process:

Initialization: Start with your input. The model suggests many next words. 
Beam search picks the 'k' best starting words/phrases.
Expansion: For each of those 'k' current best phrases, it predicts the next possible words, 
creating many new longer phrases.
Pruning: From all these new, longer phrases, it again 
selects only the 'k' absolute best, based on their total probability so far.
This process repeats until phrases are complete. It's better than greedy search because 
it explores more options, leading to more coherent results.

For Smart Compose, beam search is ideal because suggestions are usually 
short (so it's not too expensive) and consistency/accuracy are 
more important than generating wildly diverse text.
